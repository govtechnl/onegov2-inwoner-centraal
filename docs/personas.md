# Personas

Four personas guide the design of solutions in this challenge. They are
composites based on extensive user research by ICTU, not real individuals.
Use them to ground design decisions in concrete needs, language levels, and
*doenvermogen* - the capacity to act under emotional pressure.

---

## 01 · Truus, 62 - *the overwhelmed widow*

**Situation.** Truus is married to Cees (72). Cees lived in a *verzorgings­tehuis*
(care home) and died unexpectedly. Truus still lives in their shared rental
apartment.

**Demographics.**

| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 62 |
| Income | €25,000 / year (employment) |
| Household benefits | huurtoeslag, zorgtoeslag |
| Digital skill | Moderate - uses email and online banking, struggles with MijnOverheid |
| Language | Dutch (native), comfortable with everyday text, lost in legal language |

**Pain points.**

- Letters are addressed to *"de erven van Cees …"* and sent to the care
  home, the wrong address.
- Salutation feels impersonal at the worst possible moment.
- She does not know whether action is required or whether things will be
  handled automatically.
- A year after Cees's death, she is hit with a *naheffing* on the
  zorgtoeslag she never realised was being recalculated.

**What "good" looks like.**

- One personal letter that names her and explains what is being arranged
  for her, what is not, and when she should expect news.
- A predictable timeline she can refer back to.
- A way to defer obligations while she organises herself.

> *"I never thought I would feel guilty about a letter I had overlooked."*

> See [casus-truus.md](casus-truus.md) for the full letter inventory of
> Truus & Cees, organisation by organisation.

## 02 · Marcus, 48 - *the locked-out partner*

**Situation.** Marcus lost his partner suddenly to a heart attack. They had
a *geregistreerd partnerschap* (registered partnership), no joint mortgage,
no children.

**Demographics.**

| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 48 |
| Income | High, employed full-time |
| Digital skill | High: comfortable with apps, banking, MijnOverheid |
| Language | Dutch (native), fluent in legal language |

**Pain points.**

- His partner's DigiD has been revoked. He cannot log in to MijnOverheid,
  Toeslagen, or any account on his partner's BSN.
- Three calls to two different agencies yield three different answers.
- Wants a single point of contact; finds only a list of phone numbers.

**What "good" looks like.**

- A *nabestaandenmachtiging* that grants delegated, scoped, time-limited
  read access to the deceased's records across organisations.
- A consistent, authoritative answer: "this is the source of truth, this
  is what is open, this is what we are doing."

> *"I called three times and got three different answers. How am I supposed
> to know what is correct?"*

## 03 · Anneke, 71 - *the analogue widow*

**Situation.** Anneke (71) lost her husband (74). She prefers paper mail,
finds digital services intimidating, and depends on her daughter for help,
but her daughter lives in another province.

**Demographics.**

| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 71 |
| Income | AOW + small private pension |
| Digital skill | Low: has a tablet but rarely uses it for government tasks |
| Language | Dutch (native), B1 reading level, baffled by jargon |

**Pain points.**

- Cannot tell information letters from action letters at a glance.
- Tone of letters feels cold; she experiences it as the government being
  uncaring at a moment of grief.
- In a panic, throws away a letter she later turns out to need.

**What "good" looks like.**

- Letters in plain language, with a clear "what to do" box at the top.
- A trusted human contact she can call for help (or who calls her).
- Choice of channel - not a forced digital flow.

> *"I did not understand what to do. And there was no one to explain it to me."*

## 04 · The bereaved entrepreneur - *optional extension*

**Situation.** The deceased ran a sole proprietorship (*eenmanszaak*) or
freelanced (*ZZP*). Beyond the private administration, business obligations
arrive: KVK deregistration, VAT return, WBSO obligations, outstanding
invoices, RVO subsidies.

**Variable demographics.** Could be a partner of any age, with any digital
skill level. The added complexity is the entanglement of private and
business administration.

**Pain points.**

- KVK sends multiple letters to the registered business address (which may
  have been the deceased's home).
- Ongoing subsidies (RVO, WBSO) and VAT return obligations are easy to miss.
- The bereaved does not know which obligations transfer and which expire on
  death.

**What "good" looks like.**

- Business obligations surfaced in the same overview as private ones, with
  a clear distinction.
- Pro-active deregistration where it is the only sensible option.
- A "what transfers, what expires" decision aid for the bereaved.

> *"I did not even know he still had an active subsidy at RVO. I only heard
> about it six months later."*

## How to use these personas

1. **Pick a primary persona** for your prototype. You cannot serve all four
   well in a weekend.
2. **Use a secondary persona as a stress test.** If your design works for
   Truus but completely fails Anneke, that is worth knowing.
3. **Map every feature to a pain point.** If a feature does not visibly
   relieve a pain point on this list, ask whether it belongs in your demo.
